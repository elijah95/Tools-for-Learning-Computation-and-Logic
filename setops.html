Union, Intersection and Difference

The most common ways to combine sets are with the following three operations:

  1. The $\it union$ of two sets $S$ and $T$, denoted $S \cup T$, is the set containing the elements that are in $S$ or $T$, or both
  2. The $\it intersection$ of sets $S$ and $T$, written $S \cap T$, is the set containing the elements that are in both $S$ and $T$.
  3. The $\it complement$ of sets $S$ with $\it universe$ $U$, denoted $S^\complement = U \setminus S$, is the set containing those elements that are in $U$ but not in $S$.

NOTE: The universe $U$ is the set containing all elements. It's content is always constant and consists of the union of the set $S$ and its complement i.e. $U = S \cup S^\complement$.
